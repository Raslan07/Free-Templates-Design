How Each Property Works

clientWidth & clientHeight

Measures the inner visible space of an element, including its padding but excluding borders, margins, and scrollbars. It's the area where your content actually lives.

Calculation: content width/height + padding

offsetWidth & offsetHeight

Measures the full outer dimensions of an element from border to border. This includes padding, borders, and the space taken by scrollbars (if any). It does not include margins.

Calculation: content width/height + padding + border + scrollbar

scrollWidth & scrollHeight

Measures the total size of an element's content, including the parts that are not currently visible because they are outside the scrollable area. If there's no scrolling, these values are typically equal to `clientWidth` and `clientHeight`.

Calculation: Full size of all internal content, visible or not

scrollLeft & scrollTop

Represents the number of pixels that the element's content has been scrolled from its origin. A value of 0 means you are at the very top-left corner. These are the only properties on this list that you can both read and set.

clientLeft & clientTop

These properties simply give you the width of the element's left and top borders, respectively. They are useful for calculations when you need to account for the border thickness.

offsetParent, offsetLeft & offsetTop

`offsetParent` is the nearest ancestor element that has a CSS `position` value other than `static`. In our example, it's the dashed indigo box.

`offsetLeft` and `offsetTop` measure the distance from the element's outer border to the inner border of its `offsetParent`.
